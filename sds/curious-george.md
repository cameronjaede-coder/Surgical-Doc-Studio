# A Poem About Curious George

## The Little Monkey's Adventure

In a jungle far away, beneath the trees so tall,  
Lived a monkey small and brown, the most cautious of all.  
With eyes that watched and hands held close, and tail wrapped tight and still,  
George the monkey feared the world beyond his leafy hill.

But slowly, day by day, he'd peek a little more,  
At butterflies and rustling leaves he'd never noticed before.  
His eyes grew bright, his hands grew quick, his tail began to swing,  
And George the monkey started wondering about every little thing.

### The Yellow Hat

One day a man in yellow came, with hat upon his head,  
He smiled at little George and said, "Come with me instead!"  
And curious George, as was his way, could not resist the call,  
To see what lay beyond the trees, beyond the jungle wall.

### In the City

To the city George did go, where buildings touched the sky,  
Where cars and buses rumbled past, and airplanes flew up high.  
Each corner held a mystery, each door a brand new prize,  
The world was full of wonders for his small and curious eyes.

### Always Into Mischief

He painted walls he shouldn't paint, and pressed the buttons red,  
He called the fire department once, from a telephone instead.  
He floated off with balloons bright, up toward the clouds so white,  
But every mess and accident, he'd somehow make things right.

### The Man with the Yellow Hat

Through every scrape and wild escape, through chaos and through fun,  
The man in yellow stayed quite calm and never had to run.  
With patience like the ocean deep, with kindness in his heart,  
He loved his little monkey friend, right from the very start.

### The Lesson

For George taught us that questions asked are never quite in vain,  
That wonder is a precious gift, and curiosity sustains.  
Though trouble follows when we seek the answers that we crave,  
The curious heart, both bold and smart, is also kind and brave.

So here's to George, the monkey small, who taught us how to see  
That every day's an adventure when you're curious as can be.
