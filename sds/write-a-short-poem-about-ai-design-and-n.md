# Silicon Roots

In woods deep where birdsong rings,  
A server farm hums, cooling fans like wings—  
Each prompt, each pixel, each synthetic mind  
Burns watts that leave the living world behind.

We forge intelligence from silicon veins,
Teach neural webs through countless training chains,
But every insight that we coax to light
Burns carbon fuel and deepens climate's plight.

The data centers sprawl like concrete reefs,  
Consuming rivers, trading roots for griefs—  
While algorithms learn to paint a tree,  
Real saplings gasp beneath our energy.

Can we design a mind that learns to care,  
That counts the cost of carbon in the air?  
To architect not just what's fast and bright,  
But systems woven with the earth in sight?

For intelligence divorced from nature's law  
Is wisdom built on hubris, cracked and raw—  
True design remembers: we are not apart  
From soil and sky—they beat within our heart.

Let code and chlorophyll together grow,  
Let processing respect the rivers' flow,  
So when machines awaken, they may see  
The world that gave them life—and let it be.
