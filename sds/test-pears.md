# The Girl Beneath the Pear Tree

## A Pastoral Poem

---

Beneath the boughs of summer's crown,
Where golden pears hang pendant down,
A girl stands in the grass,
And watches how the moments pass.

### I. The Tree

The pear tree stands with limbs spread wide,
A ancient guardian, full of pride,
Its bark is rough like weathered stone,
Yet gentle as a grandmother's tone.

Spring painted blossoms, white as snow,
That danced when April's breezes blow,
Now autumn's hand has worked its art,
And hung sweet treasures, tart to start.

### II. The Pears

They sway like lanterns, green and gold,
Their skin so smooth, their stories old,
Each one a promise, each one sweet,
Sun-ripened, heavy, and complete.

Some freckled brown where bees have kissed,
Some perfect spheres that sun has blessed,
They cluster close or hang alone,
Like secrets that the tree has known.

### III. The Girl

She gazes up with wondering eyes,
At fruit that reaches toward the skies,
Her hands outstretched, her fingers curl,
A patient, dreaming, barefoot girl.

She knows the taste of summers past,
Of pear juice dripping, running fast,
Down sun-warmed chin and grass-stained dress,
Of sweetness, joy, and stickiness.

### IV. The Moment

The world grows still, the shadows long,
A robin sings its evening song,
The girl, the tree, the golden pears,
Are bound together, unawares.

She plucks one fruit with careful hand,
Its weight like magic, ripe and grand,
And holds it close against her heart,
Before she takes the first sweet part.

### V. The Blessing

O simple grace of tree and girl,
Of patient growth, of seasons' whirl,
Of waiting for the perfect day,
When summer gives its fruit away.

May we all stand beneath such trees,
And know such gentle mysteries,
Where time moves slow and hearts run free,
Beneath the blessed pear tree.

---

**The End**
